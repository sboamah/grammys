Are the Grammy's keeping up?

By Sylvia Boamah
The Recording Academy was founded in 1957 and formed the Grammy Awards two years after to recognize "Outstanding Achievement in the music industry". The Academy and its famed award show has had moments of controversy surrounding its lack of diversity, nominee choices, and nomination process. Are the Grammys for an audience or for its members?

Nomination process

Hover for more information.
Around 350 experts in various fields out of over 12,000 voting members determine whether or not each entry is eligible and sort submissions into the 84 categories within 30 fields/genres. Expertise is determined by what credits you have music on. (i.e. If you're a producer for a rock album and an R&B album, you are considered a rock and R&B expert).
Hover for more information.
Members can vote only in their area of expertise; up to 15 of the Grammy Awards' categories, as well as in the four all-genre general field categories (Record of the Year, Album of the Year, Song of the Year, Best New Artist).


According to the Hollywood Reporter, The Academy had 14,553 voting and “professional” members in 2020. The Grammy's nomination is heavily related to the demographic makeup of these voting members. It is directly related to expertise, as shown above. For example, if there are no female rappers as voting members, the chances for female rapper nominees are slim because of their lack of expert decision-makers.
